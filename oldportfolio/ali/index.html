by Tanja Medic

WAITING FOR ALI

On June 10, 2016, I decided to stay in my neighborhood and I met an amazing and diverse crowd.

A white woman with a portable oxygen tank - seemed like she had been involved with the civil rights movement - explained how Ali brought pride to the black community. Everybody stood tall because of him, she said. 

Black folks recalled stories about his mother and reminisced about his visits.   

A lady, native of New Orleans, reminisced about her student days when she protested against the Vietnam War, while her husband listened visibly uncomfortable. He served a tour of duty in Korea at that time.   

Professionals in their white stark shirts stood next to fast-food workers, waiting dutifully. A refugee woman from Afghanistan joined with pained expression on her face, as if she had lost her own son. 

Eastern Europeans raised their hands in unity, while Ali’s casket was passing by.

While waiting for Ali, a young woman in a white dress was handing out white flowers to the crowd. Most of the time, she stood tall and dignified like an African princess. “This is amazing,” I heard her saying. “We have a woman from Bosnia here.”

Muhammad Ali brought all of us together, and we shouldn’t forget that. May he rest in peace.
