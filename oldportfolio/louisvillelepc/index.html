Our Mission

Preventing chemical accidents by educating citizens and community groups about hazardous chemicals and hazmat prevention and protection

Emergency Alerts

In partnership with local government, our non-profit organization makes sure that Jefferson County has proper emergency alert system.

Hazards Inventory

Our voluntary committee maintains inventory reports of hazardous chemicals for all facilities in Jefferson County.

Emergency Plans

We develop emergency plans in case of accidental release of chemicals. Plans include response methods for facility owners and emergency responders.

Public Education

We provide chemical hazards data to public and first responders. In addition, we develop projects to raise awareness about chemical toxins.

Safety Fair

Every year, we organize Safety Fair to connect residents of this city with representatives from local companies and emergency groups. They get a chance to ask questions and learn more about hazmat emergency preparedness.

What You Need to Know

Why do LEPCs exist?

In December 1984, a poisonous gas killed 2,500 people at a Union Carbide plant in Bhopal, India. The gas was released accidentally.

In August 1985, a chemical leak injured six employees and sent 100 people to the hospital in Institute, West Virginia. The chemical was released accidentally at a Union Carbide plant.

To prevent and minimize harm in our own communities, Congress passed the Emergency Planning and Community Right to Know Act (EPCRA) on Oct. 17, 1986. The act is known as the Superfund Amendments and Reauthorization Act (SARA Title III). The law requires emergency planning and community right-to-know reporting on hazardous and toxic chemicals.

Right to Know

Congress imposed community right to know requirements for federal, state and local governments, tribes, and industry.

According to the law, the public has a right to know about chemicals at individual facilities, and how they are being used, stored and released into the environment.

States and communities, working with facilities, can use this information to improve chemical safety and protect public health and the environment.

Who Must Participate?

The following groups must be represented in every LEPC:

  • Local and state elected officials
  • Law enforcement
  • Emergency management
  • Fire department
  • First aid or emergency medical services
  • Health personnel
  • Local environmental agency
  • Hospitals
  • Transportation personnel
  • Broadcast and print media
  • Community groups
  • Owners and operators of regulated facilities.

Louisville LEPC

PREPARE, EDUCATE, PREVENT

Louisville LEPC makes sure that all the requirements set by Emergency Planning and Community Right to Know Act are met. It maintains the inventory reports of hazardous materials and updates emergency plan. It educates the public about chemical toxins and emergency preparedness. And it works with the local government to implement emergency alerts and shelter-in-place notifications.

Our Partners

Our Partners

ATF

The Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives is a federal law enforcement organization within the US Department of Justice. The agency investigates the unlawful use, production, and possession of firearms and explosives. They also investigate illegal trafficking of alcohol and tobacco.

American Synthetic

American Synthetic Rubber Company produces synthetic rubber, used in a car and aircraft industry. It manufactures a unique form of liquid polymer, which serves as a binding agent for the compounds used in rocket propellants. They were the sole supplier of this agent to NASA and currently supply liquid polymer to the private sector.

Arkema

The local plant produces one of Arkema’s signature products, Plexiglas acrylic resin. This resin is used in laser video disks and digital video disks.

Dow Chemical Company

Dow Chemical serves diverse industries such as agriculture, construction, electronics, energy, food, mining and plastics. The company works to improve animal nutrition and crop protection. It also makes new agricultural seeds.

DuPont

The company makes plastic additives found in bottles and containers, medical supplies, piping, window frames, car bumpers and many other products.

Earth Fare

The company sells healthy food that doesn't have high fructose corn syrup, trans fats, artificial colors and sweeteners, and synthetic growth hormones. It has a store on Summit Drive in Louisville.

Highview Fire Department

The Highview Fire District serves a population of 30,000 within a 21 square mile portion of southern Jefferson County. Highview is primarily residential with a light commercial and industrial base. Their department operates from three stations and recruits professionals and volunteers.

Indiana University SE

Indiana University Southeast is the regional campus that serves Southern Indiana and the Louisville metropolitan area. Its academic offerings include bachelor's and master’s degree programs.

KAIRE

Kentuckiana Air Education raises awareness about the benefits of clean air. KAIRE builds community involvement by encouraging individuals to make air-friendly choices in their daily lives.

KY Environmental Protection

The agency protects the environment by providing the knowledge about natural resources and establishing environmental regulations.

KY Public Health

The agency aims to improve the health and wellness of Kentucky citizens.

Lake Dreamland Fire

The Lake Dreamland Fire District covers about 17.5 square miles of the southwestern part of Jefferson County. The District has a diverse population of residential areas intermingled with industry chemical manufacturers and commercial properties.

Our Partners

Lubrizol

Lubrizol combines specialty chemicals used in fluids, vehicles, shampoos, soaps, medicine and many more everyday use items.

McMahan Fire Department

McMahan Fire Protection District is responsible for fire suppression, control of hazmat emergencies, and response to natural disasters. This fire district also helps out with emergency medical care, evacuation of civilians, clean-up of hazardous materials, and response to terrorist acts.

The National Weather Service

The National Weather Service provides weather, water, and climate data to the public. It gives forecasts and warnings about severe weather and natural disasters.

Operation Lifesaver

A non-profit organization provides public education programs in all 50 states. It aims to prevent railroad collisions, injuries and fatalities.

Paramount Safety

Paramount Safety provides training and certifications on various safety topics.

Pleasure Ridge Park Fire

The Pleasure Ridge Park Fire Protection District is one of seventeen volunteer and career departments that make up the Jefferson County Fire Service. This department covers 52 square miles of Pleasure Ridge Park and Valley Station in southwestern Jefferson County.

Rubbertown Community

The council works on developing trust between chemical companies and the Rubbertown community. It aims to improve the overall well being of the area.

Rubbertown Mutual Aid

The Rubbertown Mutual Aid Association is a coalition of companies that provide assistance and expertise to each other during plant emergencies. They were formed before local responders possessed the knowledge and equipment to successfully address chemical emergencies.

The US Coast Guard

The coast guard is a unique branch of the military responsible for maritime duties. They ensure safe and lawful commerce, and perform rescue missions in severe conditions.

YMCA Safe Place Services

The organization provides help, hope and healing to teens and families in crisis.

ZEON Chemicals

ZEON Chemicals develops polymers, synthetic elastomers and specialty chemicals that can be used in plastic bottles, nylon, paints and pressure sensitive tapes.
